Overview
Company Introduction
Jubo (Smart Ageing Tech) is a digital healthcare solution that centers on collecting, analyzing and reporting senior healthcare data. It focuses on digital solutions for long term care, bringing technologies to the traditional care industry to better connect Long-Term and Post-Acute Care (LTPAC) providers, caregivers, seniors, and families.
My Project
In Summer 2020, I joined Jubo Health as their UX design intern, focusing on redesigning the homepage as well as designing the system for organizing and sharing photos between LTPAC facilities and families.

In both projects, I went through the end-to-end design process from the beginning of user research to both internal and external user testing. As a company emphasizing on the users’ experiences, I got to collaborate with the engineering, data science, and customer success departments.

Project 1: Homepage Redesign
Original Homepage
User Research
The company is entering the adult day care industry when I started this project, hence, I conducted interviews from both long-term care and adult day care facilities.

There are two goals from this user research:
1. What do the current customers (long-term care facilities) think about our system?
2. What is the expectation from our future customers (the adult day care facilities)?
Major Insights
Goals
To use charts to display crucial, immediate, and actionable information in order to enhance work efficiency on customizable cards.
Information Architecture
I built an information architecture to better understand how I should design the overall user experience for the homepage. It also allowed me to more clearly communicate the entry and exit points as well as the relationship between each page with engineers.
Final Design
Design Reasons
The biggest change of this redesign is the use of charts. Instead of the original text-heavy design, the chart is better suited as it is able to clearly convey important information and most importantly, show the trend of the overall situation of the institutions.
“The use of color can be dangerous because it may be too strong in some cases. Think wisely when incorporating color in data visualization.” The CEO of Jubo Health said to me when he walked past during one of my brainstorming sessions. Hence, following the design styleguides of the company, I only used red when the information is required for action from the nurse or the caregivers and I used other colors to indicate the importance of each information.
To solve the problem of different institutions having different needs and each role using different information, I made the front page customizable. Hence, instead of the uneven sizes of the section based on the amount of the information, each of the sections will be identical. This will allow the developers to organize the section better.
The up to date information is a bonus point of the homepage. By connecting to different features of the app, the users will be able to know when the information was last updated and know the most immediate data.
Project 2: Photos Sharing and Organization
User Research
Through interviews at adult day care institutions, I gained in depth understanding of the process of photo organizing and sharing.
Pain Points
1. Photos can only be uploaded one by one from the local folder to Jubo’s healthcare system.

2. Some families don’t want other people to see their elders’ photos / don’t want to see other elders’ photos but all uploaded photos (either individual or group photos) will be sent to all families.
Tasks
Design a categorization system to separate individual and group photos in order to increase the efficiency of caregivers and social workers from taking, organizing, to sending correct photos to each family.
